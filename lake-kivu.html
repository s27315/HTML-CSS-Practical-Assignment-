About Lake Kivu

Lake Kivu is one of Africa's Great Lakes, sitting on the border between Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of Congo. With its crystal-clear waters, lush green hills, and charming lakeside towns like Rubavu (Gisenyi) and Karongi (Kibuye), it is the perfect destination for relaxation and adventure.

Things to Do at Lake Kivu

Boat Tours

Explore the lake's many islands and hidden coves on a scenic boat tour. Sunset cruises are especially popular with visitors.

Kayaking & Swimming

The calm, warm waters of Lake Kivu are perfect for kayaking, swimming, and paddleboarding along the scenic shoreline.

Island Hiking

Visit Napoleon Island to see thousands of fruit bats, or hike the forested hills of Amahoro Island for panoramic lake views.

Beach Relaxation

Unwind on the sandy beaches of Rubavu. Several beach clubs and resorts offer sun loungers, food, and drinks by the water.

Lakeside Towns to Visit

  • Rubavu (Gisenyi) – The most popular lakeside town, known for beaches, nightlife, and border crossing to DRC.
  • Karongi (Kibuye) – A quieter, scenic town with beautiful peninsulas and the famous Kibuye church.
  • Rusizi (Cyangugu) – A border town at the southern tip of the lake, close to Nyungwe Forest.

Travel Tips for Lake Kivu

  1. Book accommodation in advance during peak season (June–September).
  2. Hire a local boat guide for island tours — they know the best spots.
  3. Carry cash; ATMs can be scarce in smaller lakeside towns.
  4. Respect local fishing communities along the shoreline.
  5. Try fresh tilapia fish — a Lake Kivu speciality!
