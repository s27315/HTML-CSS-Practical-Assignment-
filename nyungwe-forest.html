About Nyungwe Forest

Nyungwe Forest National Park is one of the oldest rainforests in Africa, covering over 1,000 square kilometres in southwest Rwanda. It is home to chimpanzees, colobus monkeys, hundreds of bird species, and rare orchids. A visit here is an unforgettable adventure into the wild.

Top Activities

Chimpanzee Trekking

Track habituated chimpanzee groups through the dense forest with an expert guide. One of Rwanda's most thrilling wildlife experiences.

Canopy Walkway

Walk across a 160-metre suspension bridge high above the forest canopy. Breathtaking views and a unique perspective of the rainforest.

Bird Watching

With over 300 bird species including 29 Albertine Rift endemics, Nyungwe is a paradise for bird watchers from around the world.

Nature Hiking

Choose from over 13 well-marked trails ranging from easy walks to challenging full-day hikes through the heart of the forest.

Wildlife You Might See

  • 🐒 Chimpanzees
  • 🐾 Angola Colobus Monkeys
  • 🦜 Grey Crowned Crane (Rwanda's national bird)
  • 🦋 Rare butterflies and orchids
  • 🐍 Forest cobras and chameleons
  • 🐘 Forest elephants (rare sightings)

Visitor Information

Location Southwest Rwanda, near Cyangugu
Park Entry Fee $40 USD per person

Best Time to Visit June – September (dry season)
Distance from Kigali ~225 km (approx. 5 hours drive)
Guided Tours Available daily from park headquarters

What to Pack

  1. Waterproof hiking boots
  2. Long-sleeved shirts and trousers
  3. Insect repellent and sunscreen
  4. Rain jacket or poncho
  5. Binoculars for bird watching
  6. Camera with extra batteries
  7. Enough water and snacks for the trail
